Welcome to Carson Phillip's Home Page

About Me

Hi, I’m Carson Phillips! I’m a high school senior with big dreams of becoming a world-renowned journalist and editor-in-chief of The New Yorker. Born and raised in a small, suffocating town, I’ve always felt out of place in a world full of mediocrity. I’m ambitious, unapologetically sarcastic, and determined to escape my small-town existence to achieve greatness. Writing is my true passion, and I love telling stories that matter. I’m not afraid to speak my mind, even if it makes me a little unpopular. Besides writing, I enjoy reading, critiquing people (constructively, of course), and plotting my future far away from here.

What Im Working On

Right now, I’m working on creating a literary magazine to showcase my peers’ (mostly unwilling) contributions. It’s part of my master plan to strengthen my college applications and get accepted into Northwestern University’s journalism program. It’s a bit of a challenge since most of my classmates couldn’t care less about writing—or anything intellectual—but I’m using some creative tactics (read: blackmail) to make it happen.

My Goals

My ultimate goal is to leave this town and never look back. I want to attend Northwestern University, major in journalism, and eventually become one of the greatest writers of my generation. Someday, I’ll be running my own publication or working as an editor for The New Yorker, crafting stories that inspire, provoke, and leave a lasting impact. Until then, my short-term goal is simple: survive high school, get accepted into college, and prove to everyone who doubted me that I’m destined for greatness.
